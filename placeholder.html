The Aurora Ribbed Beanie

A chic and cozy knit by The Woolly Alchemist

The Aurora Ribbed Beanie is a cozy, stretchy hat designed with warmth and elegance in mind, featuring a snug 2x2 ribbed fabric and neatly shaped crown. Perfect for knitters comfortable with basic knitting in the round, this project utilizes ribbing and simple k2tog decreases. It's an excellent choice for a quick gift or a stylish addition to your own wardrobe.

Skill Level: Adventurous Beginner | Estimated Time: 4-7 hours

+Introduction & Notes

Welcome to the Aurora Beanie pattern. This design emphasizes clarity and an enjoyable making process. Please read through the entire pattern before beginning.

Key Pattern Notes:

  • The hat is worked in the round from the bottom up.
  • Sized S (M, L, XL). Numbers are given as S (M, L, XL) e.g., (110 sts). If only one number is provided, it applies to all sizes. The selected size's numbers will be bolded in the instructions.
  • Slip the BOR marker on every round.
  • Gauge is critical. Adjust needle size if your gauge differs. More stitches per inch means you need larger needles; fewer stitches per inch means smaller needles.
  • For sizes S and L, the 2x2 rib at the join will be slightly offset (ending with K2). This is generally not noticeable. Sizes M and XL will end the rib round with P2.

+Sizing Information

This beanie is designed in 4 sizes: S (M, L, XL). The sample shown is size L on a model with a 22.5-inch head.

Fit Notes: This hat is designed with approximately 2-3 inches (5-7.5 cm) of negative ease for a snug fit. The 2x2 ribbed fabric is very stretchy and will accommodate a range of head sizes within each listed size.

Select your size based on the 'Fits Head Circumference' in the table below. If between sizes, consider sizing down for a very snug fit or sizing up for a slightly more relaxed (but still fitted) feel.

+Materials

  • Yarn: Worsted weight yarn (CYC 4). Approx. 160 yds / 146 m. (See Sizing Table for specific amounts per size).
    Recommended: Soft merino or alpaca blend (e.g., Malabrigo Worsted, Rowan Pure Wool Worsted).
    Yarn Substitution Notes: Any worsted-weight yarn that achieves gauge and has good elasticity can be used. Wool or wool blends are recommended for warmth, stretch, and stitch definition in the ribbing.
  • Needles: - US 7 (4.5 mm) circular needles, 16-inch (40 cm) cord (for brim).
    - US 8 (5.0 mm) circular needles, 16-inch (40 cm) cord (for body).
    - US 8 (5.0 mm) DPNs or preferred method for small circumference knitting (e.g., Magic Loop) for crown.
  • Notions: Stitch marker (for BOR), tapestry needle, scissors. Optional: pom-pom maker.
  • Gauge: 20 sts x 26 rnds = 4 inches (10 cm) in K2, P2 ribbing using US 8 (5.0 mm) needles, worked in the round, measured unstretched after blocking.
    To Swatch: CO at least 28-32 sts (a multiple of 4 for K2P2 rib). Work in K2, P2 rib in the round for at least 4 inches in height. Block your swatch as you intend to block the final hat. Once dry, lay flat and measure 4 inches in the center of the swatch, away from edges, without stretching. Count the number of stitches and rows within that 4-inch area.

Pattern Instructions

+Instructions

1. Brim (2x2 Rib)

Using US 7 (4.5 mm) circular needles and a stretchy cast-on method (e.g., Long Tail CO):

  1. CO 110 sts.
  2. Place BOR marker and join for working in the round, being careful not to twist stitches.
  3. Set-up Rnd: *K2, p2; rep from * to end of rnd.
  4. Continue in 2x2 rib until brim measures 2 inches (approx. 5 cm) from CO edge, or desired length. For a folded brim, work for 3.5-4 inches.
For a smoother transition to the body, knit all stitches on the last round of ribbing (optional).

2. Body (Stockinette Stitch)

  1. Change to US 8 (5.0 mm) circular needles.
  2. Main Body Rnd: Knit all sts.
  3. Continue in Stockinette stitch (knit every rnd) until the entire piece measures 6.5 inches (approx. 16.5 cm) from the CO edge (including brim).
    (This is approx. 4.5 inches / 11.5 cm of Stockinette stitch after the brim).
Checkpoint: Ensure hat is at the correct height before starting crown decreases.

3. Crown Shaping

Switch to DPNs or preferred small-circumference method when stitches become too tight on circular needles (typically after a few decrease rounds).

Decrease rounds are detailed below. Stitch counts are for S (M, L, XL); your selected size's values will be bolded.

  1. Dec Rnd 1: *K9, k2tog; rep from * to end. 100
  2. Next Rnd: Knit.
  3. Dec Rnd 2: *K8, k2tog; rep from * to end. 90
  4. Next Rnd: Knit.
  5. Dec Rnd 3: *K7, k2tog; rep from * to end. 80
  6. Next Rnd: Knit.
  7. Dec Rnd 4: *K6, k2tog; rep from * to end. 70
  8. Next Rnd: Knit.
  9. Dec Rnd 5: *K5, k2tog; rep from * to end. 60
  10. Next Rnd: Knit.
  11. Dec Rnd 6: *K4, k2tog; rep from * to end. 50
  12. Next Rnd: Knit.
  13. Dec Rnd 7: *K3, k2tog; rep from * to end. 40
  14. Next Rnd: Knit.
  15. Dec Rnd 8: *K2, k2tog; rep from * to end. 30
  16. Dec Rnd 9: *K1, k2tog; rep from * to end. 20
  17. Dec Rnd 10: *K0, k2tog (i.e., k2tog); rep from * to end. 10
  18. Dec Rnd 11 (XL Only): *K2tog; rep from * to end. 10
When using DPNs, pull the yarn slightly tighter on the first stitch of each new needle to prevent ladders.

+Finishing

  1. When 10 sts rem: Cut yarn, leaving an 8-10 inch tail.
  2. Thread tail onto a tapestry needle. Pass needle through rem 10 sts as if to purl, slipping them off DPNs.
  3. Pull tail firmly to close the top opening.
  4. Bring yarn tail to the inside of the hat. Secure with a few knots and weave in the end.
  5. Weave in the CO tail at the brim. [Video Tutorials]
  6. Blocking (Recommended): Gently wash beanie according to yarn care instructions. Squeeze out excess water (do not wring). Lay flat to dry, or shape over a balloon or bowl for a rounded crown. Blocking evens out stitches.
  7. Optional: Add a pom-pom.

+Required Skills

This pattern utilizes the following knitting techniques. Video links can be found in the Support section.

  • Long-Tail Cast-On: A common, stretchy method for casting on stitches.
  • Knitting in the Round: Creating a seamless tube of fabric using circular or double-pointed needles. Ensure stitches are not twisted when joining.
  • Knit (k) & Purl (p) stitches: The fundamental stitches of knitting.
  • 2x2 Ribbing: A pattern of *K2, P2* repeated to create a stretchy, textured fabric.
  • Stockinette Stitch (in the round): Achieved by knitting all stitches of every round.
  • Knit 2 Together (k2tog): A common right-leaning decrease, reducing stitch count by one.
  • Working with DPNs / Magic Loop: Methods for knitting small circumferences, such as the crown of a hat.
  • Weaving in Ends: Neatly securing yarn tails into the fabric.
  • Blocking: Wetting or steaming finished knitting to even out stitches and achieve final dimensions.
